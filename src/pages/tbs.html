Background

My team was tasked with redesigning Aon's signature online benefits platform after client feedback indicated that the existing user experience is confusing and difficult to navigate. Our clients' HR departments are overwhelmed with requests for guidance. As a result, there is concern that competitors that center user experience will threaten Aon’s market share.

Goals

  • Simplify the user experience by addressing friction points and offering key information at the right times. Success will look like a reduction in poor feedback and customer service calls.
  • Boost year-round engagement through new wellness and financial tools. Success will look like increased usage overall and usage of these tools.
  • Provide employers with a centralized platform to demonstrate the value of their benefits, thus increasing employee retention.

Challenges and Constraints

  • Reducing friction around benefits enrollment, without being able to structure the carrier plans.
  • Demonstrating the value of benefits and helping users choose the options that best meet their individual needs.
  • Tailoring the platform for the Asia Pacific, Europe, and Middle East markets.
  • Building in flexibility to account for individual client configuration.

User Feedback

After completing our proof of concept, we conducted user testing on the updated benefit enrolment flow.

97% of users in Asia Pacific and 98% of users in the Europe and Middle East said the experience was simpler than before.

98% of users in all markets felt the process was significantly improved.

Users primarily care about the ease of executing core tasks, such as filing a claim and enrolling in benefits.

The market is saturated with lifestyle apps and tools. Users don’t want more apps, but they are interested in aggregating their data onto a single platform.

Customization

For our clients, it's very important that TBS reflects their brands. In the past, Aon's configuration team wrote custom CSS for every individual client site. Our clients appreciated the flexibility of this approach, but it's a time-consuming process, and the resulting color combinations rarely meet accessibility criteria.

Delivery (Ongoing)

Benefit Selection

When we revisited the enrolment flow in delivery, we knew we needed to rethink the step at which the user selects their benefits. Our original proof of concept design (left) showed all of the benefits in every category on one page. This was overwhelming for the user. In delivery (right), we present only one category of benefits on a page at a time, thus reducing cognitive overload.
