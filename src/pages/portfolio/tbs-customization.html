Background

Aon's second largest vertical is insurance brokering, which means we work with client companies and insurance carriers to negotiate employee benefits packages. As part of this service, we offer our client companies TBS, an online benefits portal through which their employees can enroll in benefits. TBS serves 17,000 clients and accounts for 1.4 billion in revenue.

Our clients invest a great deal of time and money into articulating their visual identities, messaging, and values, and they want their employees to see these elements of their brand when they log into TBS. In the past, Aon's configuration team wrote custom CSS for every individual client site. This was an inefficient and time-consuming process, and the resulting color combinations rarely met accessibility criteria.

Customization Strategy

We took a unique approach to customization that gives clients enormous flexibility within a structured system:

  • Theme color: Clients can select from one of eight theme colors.
  • Brand color: Clients can also opt to apply their brand color in addition to the theme color. In some places, the brand color will override the theme color. (We still use the theme palette for interactive elements, which have special accessibility requirements.) The method of applying the brand color varies depending on whether it is a light or dark hue.
  • Brand assets: Additionally, clients supply their own brand assets, such as photography and iconography.
  • Light and Dark Mode: Lastly, clients can choose to have their site in either light or dark mode.

Initially, our business stakeholders were concerned that existing clients would be dissatisfied by the constraints placed on them by this structured approach. However, we have since demoed it to several existing clients, and all of them have been very satisfied with the results.

Branding

Client Brand Color

If a client supplies their brand color, we apply it to select components. We have two different approaches to brand color application, depending on whether it's a darker or brighter hue.

Visual Assets

We also encourage clients to provide their own photography, icons, and other brand assets.
