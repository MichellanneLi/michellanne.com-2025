Background

The existing online benefits platform is confusing and difficult to navigate. Client HR departments are overwhelmed with requests for guidance. Competitors that center user experience threaten Aon’s market share.

Goals

  • Simplify the user experience by addressing friction points and offering key information at the right times. Success will look like a reduction in poor feedback and customer service calls.
  • Boost year-round engagement through new wellness and financial tools. Success will look like increased usage overall and usage of these tools.
  • Provide employers with a centralized platform to demonstrate the value of their benefits, thus increasing employee retention.

Challenges and Constraints

  • Reducing friction around benefits enrollment, without being able to structure the carrier plans.
  • Demonstrating the value of benefits and helping users choose the options that best meet their individual needs.
  • Tailoring the platform for the Asia Pacific, Europe, and Middle East markets.
  • Building in sufficient flexibility to account for individual client configuration.

Timeline

2020

  • Proof of concept prototypes delivered for UK and Singapore markets.
  • Concept and viability testing complete for Asia Pacific, Europe, and the Middle East, using either in-person prototype demonstration or video demonstration, followed by a survey.

Proof of Concept

User Dashboard

The user dashboard brings together all of the platform’s tools in one place.

In user testing, employees liked that there are fewer clicks to access content compared to the existing platform.

{{> project-image style='' classes='has-shadow width-100' img='tbs/poc/tbs-dashboard-01.png' alt='tbs dashboard' caption='TBS Dashboard'}}

Each “module” represents a tool, such as:

  • Personal Finance,
  • Total Rewards (or compensation),
  • Pensions, and
  • Health Score

Pensions and health tracking are new features we introduced to boost engagment. However, employees told us they are already using apps to track these types of metrics, so our additions have to compete or integrate with trackers such as Fitbit.

{{> project-image style='' classes='has-shadow' img='tbs/poc/tbs-dashboard-top.png' alt='finance, rewards, and pension modules on the tbs dashboard' caption='Finance, Rewards, Pensions, and Health modules'}}

User can access both their current benefits and available anytime benefits. In user testing, this was highly valued in both the APAC and EMEA markets.

“Separation of the Anytime benefits and My benefits is a game changer.”

User Feedback

After completing our proof of concept, we conducted user testing on the updated benefit enrolment flow.

97% of users in Asia Pacific and 98% of users in the Europe and Middle East said the experience was simpler than before.

98% of users in all markets felt the process was significantly improved.

Users primarily care about the ease of executing core tasks, such as filing a claim and enrolling in benefits.

The market is saturated with lifestyle apps and tools. Users don’t want more apps, but they are interested in aggregating their data onto a single platform.
