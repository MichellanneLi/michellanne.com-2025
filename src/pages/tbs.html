Background

My team was tasked with redesigning Aon's signature online benefits platform after client feedback indicated that the existing user experience is confusing and difficult to navigate. Our clients' HR departments are overwhelmed with requests for guidance. As a result, there is concern that competitors that center user experience will threaten Aon’s market share.

Goals

  • Simplify the user experience by addressing friction points and offering key information at the right times. Success criteria: a reduction in poor feedback and customer service calls.
  • Boost year-round engagement through new wellness and financial tools. Success criteria: increased usage overall and usage of these tools.
  • Provide employers with a centralized platform to demonstrate the value of their benefits. Success criteria: improved employee retention.

Challenges and Constraints

  • Reducing friction around benefits enrollment, without being able to structure the carrier plans.
  • Demonstrating the value of benefits and helping users choose the options that best meet their individual needs.
  • Tailoring the platform for the Asia Pacific, Europe, and Middle East markets.
  • Building in flexibility to account for individual client configuration.

Annual Enrollment

During the annual enrollment window, the user is prompted to enroll in next year’s benefits. Historically, the enrollment flow took a "hub and spoke" approach. The user journey began on a "shopfront" page displaying all available benefits. The user would engage with each one separately. After enrolling in a benefit, they would be directed back to the "shopfront". In order to finalize their benefit selections, the user needed to submit all of them from the "shopfront" page.

This flow caused a lot of confusion. We learned from clients that employees routinely did not finalize their benefit selections because they thought they had already completed enrollment. Users told us that they had trouble understanding the steps in the enrollment process.

{{> project-image style='' classes='has-shadow' img='tbs/poc/tbs-enrolment-flow--old.png' alt='' caption=''}}

We therefore proposed a linear flow, featuring a progress bar and persistent CTAs at the bottom of the page to make the next steps clear. The user begins by selecting the benefits they want to engage with, and they are guided through the process of enrolling in each of them.

{{> project-image style='' classes='has-shadow' img='tbs/poc/tbs-enrolment-flow--new-1.png' alt='' caption=''}}

After completing our proof of concept, we conducted user testing on the updated benefit enrolment flow.

97% of users in Asia Pacific and 98% of users in the Europe and Middle East said the experience was simpler than before.

98% of users in all markets felt the process was significantly improved.

Benefit Selection

When we revisited the enrolment flow in delivery, we knew we needed to rethink the step at which the user selects their benefits. Our original proof of concept design (left) showed all of the benefits in every category on one page. This was overwhelming for the user. In delivery (right), we present only one category of benefits on a page at a time, thus reducing cognitive overload. This adds pages and steps to the enrollment flow, but we feel the trade-off is worth it for clients that offer dozens of benefits (clients that offer very few benefits will still display all of them on a single page.)

Benefit Taxonomy

When a user enrolls in a benefit, the plan options available to them depend on how the carrier has structured the benefit. Because Aon brokers countless benefits with different carriers across dozens of markets, we needed to create a flexible system of displaying plan options that would work for every possible benefit permutation. This challenge is unique to TBS and sets it apart from our smaller e-commerce benefits platforms.

We organized the benefit structures into categories: tiered, currency, and volume. Each benefit option is expressed as a discrete plan card that shows different information depending on the benefit structure.

All plan cards display:

  • the plan title,
  • a description,
  • participants (if relevant),
  • costs,
  • and a button to select the plan.

Additional information can be accessed by expanding the "More Details" and "Cost Summary" sections.

In addition to the cost to the employee, we also display the cost to the employer. This is important to our clients because it demonstrates their investment in their employees' wellbeing. We also display the combined total cost because it has tax implications in the UK market.
